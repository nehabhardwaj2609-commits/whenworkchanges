Reading list & writing

The research that
shapes this work.

Six pieces worth reading if you want to understand what's happening — and why it matters beyond the headlines. Plus writing from Neha as the research progresses.

Reading list

Six pieces worth your time.

Chosen because each one says something true — and because they might give language to something you've been feeling.

01
A World Without Work
Daniel Susskind — 2020

The most important book on AI and employment of recent years. Susskind introduces the concept of "task encroachment" — the slow, relentless process by which machines take on more and more of what humans do. His argument: this time really is different. And the identity mismatch — when available jobs don't match who people feel they are — is one of the least-discussed but most damaging consequences.

Find out more →
02
Artificial Intelligence and the Future of Work: A Functional-Identity Perspective
Selenko, Bankins, Shoss et al. — 2022

The first major framework for understanding what AI does to people's sense of self at work — not just their employment status. Whether AI threatens or expands a worker's identity depends on how it's deployed and which tasks it takes over. Essential reading for HR practitioners and coaches who want to understand what's actually happening to people.

Read the paper →
03
What Generative AI Means for the Media Industries
Guzman & Lewis — 2024

Why communications professionals specifically? Guzman and Lewis make the case that generative AI is different from previous technologies because it steps into the creator role — producing the content that media and comms professionals have always defined themselves by. The disruption is not to the tools. It is to the identity of the profession itself.

Read the paper →
04
Job Insecurity and Its Consequences: Addressing Unanswered Questions
Selenko, Mäkikangas & Stride — 2013

A finding most people miss: job insecurity alone — before any actual job loss — is enough to undermine professional self-concept. The psychological damage doesn't wait for a redundancy notice. If you've felt it before anything formal happened, this paper names why — and why it's real, not just anxiety.

Read the paper →
05
Working Identity: Unconventional Strategies for Reinventing Your Career
Herminia Ibarra — 2003

Ibarra's foundational work on career transition and identity. Her central insight — that identity change requires acting first and reflecting later, through "provisional selves" — is directly relevant to anyone navigating a major professional shift. Practical, readable, and grounded in real stories. Still the most useful book on what reinvention actually looks like from the inside.

Find out more →
06
Anticipatory AI-Related Distress (AIRD): Emerging Evidence and Implications
McNamara & Thornton — 2025

Brand new research naming something many people are already experiencing but lack language for: anticipatory distress about what AI might do to their career, before anything has formally changed. If you've felt anxious about your professional future in ways that are hard to explain to your manager — this research describes why it's legitimate, and why it matters.
