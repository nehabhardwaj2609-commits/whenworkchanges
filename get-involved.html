About the researcher

Person first. Researcher second.

Neha Bhardwaj whenworkchanges.co.nz

I'm Neha Bhardwaj. I've spent over twenty years in HR, organisational change, and coaching. I've sat in the rooms where restructures get planned. I know what those conversations look like from the inside — and I carry that into this research.

I chose my own name at age five. Identity has never been abstract to me.

I'm doing this Masters because I've watched too many people go through change that could have been handled better. With AI, that's going to get much bigger. I want to understand what's really happening to people — so the frameworks, the coaching, the support actually helps.

Masters of Technological Futures, Generation 27 — AcademyEX, Aotearoa New Zealand.

What participation involves

One conversation. Your story, in your words.

No commitment until you decide. No surprises once you do.

1
You reach out
Email me or use the contact below. No commitment — just letting me know you're interested.
2
We have a kōrero first
A short phone call — 15 to 20 minutes — where I explain the research, you can ask any questions, and we can both decide if this feels right. Relationship before data.
3
The research conversation
60 to 90 minutes. Video call or in person — wherever you're comfortable. I'll guide us through, but you lead the story. We'll also use a simple visual journey map together.
4
After we talk
I'll follow up to check in. A small koha is offered as a genuine thank you for your time and trust. You may also be invited to review findings later — entirely your call.
◈
Fully confidential
Nothing you share will be identifiable in any research output
◈
Ethics approved
Reviewed and approved by the AcademyEX ethics panel
◈
Withdraw anytime
For any reason, at any point, no questions asked
